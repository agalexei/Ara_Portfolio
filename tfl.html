Introduction

Trees For Life was established in 1981 and remains a vibrant community organisation of 7,000 active supporters. Trees For Life delivers conservation, revegetation, community engagement and training programs and services.

The Problem

The website needs a little polishing and clearer, simpler user flows, as well as new functionality to increase community engagement.

The Solution

A redesigned homepage that better communicates Trees for Life’s mission to visitors and new pages with a new workshop sign-up feature and a simplified donation process.

-

Stakeholder Interview

By intervirwing the stakeholder, we identified key areas of improvement on the site:

1. Too many different pages and too much text
2. Needs to be more minimalistic and visually appealing
3. Needs an online booking systems for volunteering opportunities
4. A bigger emphasis on TFL being a South Australian not-for-profit organisation

-

User Research

Key insights from surveys were:

A. Moajority of the participants were aged 25- 44 and already involved in a Non-Profit
B. Attending a workshop and donating were preferred options as a first step in engaging with Trees for Life
C. Users had issues with scheduling volunteering opportunities and wanted a booking systems for volunteering opportunities
D. A bigger emphasis on Trees For Life being a South Australian not-for-profit organisation

-

Interviews and Testing

Key insights from interviews and testing were:

1. In order for volunteers to stay motivated, they'd like to see a difference from their impacts
2. Easier ways to sign-up and book workshops and volunteer days
3. Receiving confirmations and calendar invites for workshops in order to not forget
4. An easier and secure "checkout" experience when making donations online
5. Better content chunking

-

Defining a problem statement

"Problem Statement: Trees for Life (TFL) aims to improve the health and extent of South Australia’s natural landscapes. To do this, TFL needs to recruit more volunteers to be involved in nature restoration and collect donations to fund these efforts. In order to engage more volunteers and attract donations, TFL needs to demonstrate the impact they are having and make it easy and straightforward for people to donate or volunteer. The current TFL website is very text heavy and can give visitors a sense of information overload, which may be a barrier to participation for some visitors. TFL needs to simplify their opportunities and streamline the donation and volunteer sign-up processes to better meet their goals."

Iterations

No automatic donation frequency, as user may unknowingly subscribe to payments.
