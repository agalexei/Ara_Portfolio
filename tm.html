Introduction

When it comes to dating apps, you’ve got options: Badoo, Bumble, Hinge, Match, POF, OKCupid and many more. It doesn’t matter if you want to find love, a date, or just have a casual chat, you want an app that’s the right match for you. And it’s not always black and white – when you want to meet new people, your friends at Tinder can help you out with features designed to make the impossible possible

The Problem

People feel online dating is very superficial and this is causing our users to get frustrated and ghosted.

How might we improve the user’s experience with online dating more enjoyable, while allowing people to make serious connections?

The Solution

Tinder Matchmaker is designed to achieve more meaningful connections for our existing users by involving more people in the process and make meeting Tinder matches more fun and interesting.

-

User Research

Key insights from research was:

53% of the people surveyed have previously used dating apps but no longer do.
Ages 24-35 our research showed that the majority of dating app users fall within this age bracket.
Female Users believed that dating apps catered more for men's "dating" criteria than their's.
Men and Women of participants are concerned about food wastage and their carbon footprint

-

Interviews and Testing

Key insights from interviews and testing were:

1. Through user research we have discovered we can enhance users experience of dating apps by involving more people in the process.
2. BUsers sometimes feel frustrated and disposable with the superficial nature of the swipe right culture, they worry about safety and encounter matches disappearing without explanation (ghosting).
3. Allowing the users' friends to take an active role in the matchmaking process will solve the problems of ghosting, enhance safety and ultimately enable users to make more meaningful connections with people who have similar life goals which will lead to them finding a long term relationship.

-

Defining a problem statement

Tinder Matchmaker is designed to achieve more meaningful connections for our existing users and make meeting Tinder matches more fun and interesting. We have observed that Tinder isn't achieving this goal and users feel online dating is very superficial. This is causing our users to get frustrated and ghosted. Ultimately, moving to competitor apps. How might we enhance our users' experience by allowing the users' friends to take an active role in the matchmaking process. This in turn will allow users to make more meaningful connections and solve the issue of users being ghosted.
